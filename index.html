Privacy Policy Last updated: May 30, 2025 Short version: GameCard is a local desktop application. We do not operate servers, we do not collect your data, and nothing you do in GameCard is transmitted to us. Ever. 1. What GameCard Is GameCard is a desktop application for Windows that allows gamers to format, organize, and publish their gameplay clips. The app runs entirely on your local machine. There is no GameCard account, no GameCard server, and no GameCard cloud storage. 2. Data We Collect GameCard collects no personal data. The app does not transmit any information to GameCard or any third party operated by GameCard. All data created within GameCard — including clip metadata, preset configurations, profile information, and social media tokens — is stored exclusively in your local application data folder on your device. 3. YouTube Data API Usage GameCard uses the YouTube Data API v3 to allow users to upload gameplay clips directly to their own YouTube channel from within the app. GameCard requests the youtube.upload scope, which allows the app to upload videos on your behalf. Your YouTube OAuth tokens (which authorize uploads) are stored only on your local device in the app's local storage. They are never transmitted to GameCard or any GameCard server. GameCard does not read, access, or store any data from your YouTube account other than your channel name (displayed in Settings to confirm which account is connected). GameCard does not share your YouTube credentials or tokens with any third party. You can disconnect your YouTube account at any time via Settings → YouTube Shorts → Disconnect, which immediately deletes the stored tokens from your device. GameCard's use of YouTube Data API Services is also subject to the Google Privacy Policy. By connecting your YouTube account, you also agree to the YouTube Terms of Service. 4. Third-Party Services GameCard integrates with the following third-party services at the user's direction: YouTube Data API v3 — for uploading clips to the user's YouTube channel tracker.gg — displayed in an embedded web view to show game statistics. GameCard does not collect or transmit any data from this view. Riot Games API — optionally used to display Valorant statistics. API keys are stored locally on the user's device only. These services have their own privacy policies. GameCard is not responsible for the data practices of third-party services. 5. Local Storage GameCard stores the following data locally on your device only: Clip metadata (file paths, names, tags, preset assignments) Preset configurations (zone layouts, social caption templates) Profile information you enter (username, rank, region, social links) YouTube OAuth tokens (if you connect your YouTube account) Riot Games API key (if you choose to enter one) Export folder preferences and app configuration None of this data is ever transmitted to GameCard or any third party. 6. Clip Files GameCard accesses video files on your device solely to display, format, and export them at your direction. GameCard does not upload your video files to any GameCard server. When you choose to post a clip to YouTube, the video is uploaded directly from your device to YouTube using the YouTube Data API. 7. Children's Privacy GameCard is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. 8. Continued use of GameCard after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. 9. Contact If you have questions about this privacy policy, you can reach us at: gamecardnetwork@gmail.com
